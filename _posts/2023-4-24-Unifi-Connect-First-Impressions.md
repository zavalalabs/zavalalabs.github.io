# Tl;dr

New tech and emerging technologies tend to draw me in the most. Especially technologies that have the home approval factor. That home approval factor is one key reason I started looking into Unifi Connect. If it can beat other DIY solutions like DakBoard, I'm in for investing some cash. *Turns out its nothing like what my own imagination thought it would be.* There is a learning curve so be aware if you like the idea of Dakboard Smart mirrors, you might not like Unifi Connect right off. 

# Digging into Unifi Connect

Once I saw the Unifi Connect product video in my youtube feed I was hooked. I had to have it. I had to try it. I had to see if it would work for me. But the question you might have is, what is Unifi Connect? 
### What is it?
I've linked the video below for you to watch. The base idea is that Unifi Connect started out as an Enterprise Internet of Things platform. When the EA program launched its first products in the line, it started with digital displays. The idea here is that Ubiquiti is trying to provide a solution that will eventually have greater functionality. Some items featured in the video hint at a portal for employees to access door access, wifi, meeting rooms and other things yet to come.

## My first impressions

Did I jump in head first? Short answer Yes! Within a few days, I downloaded the application on the Unifi CloudKey Gen2 and started poking around the interface. What a baron wasteland! Almost no insight into what this application was able to do, and as with any new product from Unifi, you cant gain much insight until you have a physical product in hand to play with the solution. 

Purchasing the EA 7-inch device was a good start, no way did I want to purchase a larger display that I may not even be able to use should the service not meet my own needs. It's a good thing I did not purchase the larger display as I would later learn.
