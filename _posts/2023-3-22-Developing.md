# What's a setup?

For each person, setup means different things. In this post, I want to focus on "Office Setup" and "Home Setup" and how we can make our lives easier in this new world of remote work. However, before we get into that, let's talk about and stress the importance of working with your IT department on what you can do or use. Each IT organization is different and has different policies around what they allow and don't allow. Hopefully, I will shed some light on the subject in this post, so you can better understand what you can and can't do or why your IT department might not be allowing you to do something.

## The why? Behind the IT department's rules.

On youtube, all you have to do is a quick search to see so many "Work from home" setups and how some of the coolest tech gets used. I won't lie, I have been sucked in on wanting to buy some of this cool tech, but not all that tech you see in the clips will work for you as an individual with your limitations and needs. 

I am sure countless IT admins look at these videos and say, "Oh, my users better not get that," or "That vendor can't be allowed; it's too grey-boxed," and I'm sure countless other things can be said. So let's break down a few of the things you need to consider when looking at these videos.

### Input Devices

Okay, so let's start with one of the most important things you need to consider when getting input devices. (These include keyboards, mice, trackpads, etc.) First and foremost, before you add to your cart any of the cool keyboards that make the most noise (or the least), some items to consider are:

1. Does this keyboard come from a trusted vendor?
2. Does this keyboard need proprietary software to work
3. Is the price worth it for your wallet? 
4. What would you do if your IT department blocked it?
